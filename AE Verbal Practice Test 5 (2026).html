1. Antonyms

MERCURIAL
Options:
  • A. level–headed
  • B. ephemeral
  • C. laconic
  • D. Skinflint
Correct Answer: A
Explanation: Mercurial means unpredictable so antonym will be level-headed which means calm and sensible even in difficult situations. 

2. Antonyms

AUTHENTIC
Options:
  • A. Genuine
  • B. Reliable
  • C. Valid
  • D. Fictitious
Correct Answer: D
Explanation: "Authentic" means genuine or real, while "fictitious" means imaginary or not real.

3. Antonyms

BRITTLE
Options:
  • A. Strong
  • B. Frail
  • C. Fragile
  • D. Dismal
Correct Answer: A
Explanation: "Brittle" means fragile or easily broken, while "strong" indicates the opposite, meaning sturdy or durable.

4. Antonyms

COMPASSION
Options:
  • A. Kindness
  • B. Sympathy
  • C. Cruelty
  • D. Empathy
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: "Compassion" refers to the feeling of sympathy and concern for the sufferings or misfortunes of others, while "cruelty" indicates a lack of compassion or kindness.

5. Antonyms

DISASTER
Options:
  • A. Calamity
  • B. Prosperity
  • C. Misfortune
  • D. Mishap
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: "Disaster" refers to a sudden event causing great damage or suffering, while "prosperity" indicates a state of flourishing or successfulness.

6. Antonyms

FATIGUE
Options:
  • A. Vigour
  • B. Exhausted
  • C. Tiredness
  • D. Weakness
Correct Answer: A
Explanation: "Fatigue" refers to tiredness or exhaustion, while "vigour" indicates strength, energy, or vitality.

7. Antonyms

MODERATE
Options:
  • A. limited
  • B. Reasonable
  • C. Lenient
  • D. Excessive
Correct Answer: D
Explanation: "Moderate" means within reasonable limits or not excessive, while "excessive" means exceeding the bounds of moderation or reasonableness.

8. Antonyms

At first the workers were agreeable to the proposals of their Manager, but later they were reconciled to the new proposals.
Options:
  • A. resistant
  • B. estranged
  • C. cooperate
  • D. agreed 
Correct Answer: A
Explanation: "Reconciled" means accepting or coming to terms with something, while "resistant" means being opposed or unwilling to accept something. The other options do not convey the opposite meaning of "reconciled" as accurately.

9. Antonyms

The thief showed his disbelief when informed that his partner had been arrested.
Options:
  • A. displayed
  • B. concealed
  • C. battled
  • D. marked
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: "Concealed" is the direct antonym of "showed," meaning to hide or keep something hidden, which is the opposite of making something visible or displaying it. The other options do not provide a meaning that directly opposes "showed."

10. Antonyms

The movie star's biography is a glossy, sycophantic portrayal.
Options:
  • A. cowardly
  • B. domineering
  • C. flattering
  • D. insolent
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: "Sycophantic" means excessively flattering, usually to gain favor, which matches the meaning of "flattering" closely. The other options do not convey the same sense of insincere praise.

11. Antonyms

The Minister unleashed a compliment against the newspaper for its biased editorial on illiteracy among women in his constituency.
Options:
  • A. denunciation
  • B. endorsement
  • C. regulation
  • D. speculation 
Correct Answer: A
Explanation: "Compliment" means to praise, while "denunciation" means to publicly condemn or criticize, making it the correct antonym. The other options do not directly oppose the meaning of "compliment."

12. Antonyms

Choose the correct ANTONYM for sullen from the options given below. 
Options:
  • A. morose
  • B. reticent
  • C. timid
  • D. genial
Correct Answer: D
Explanation: "Genial" is the opposite of "sullen" because it means friendly and cheerful, whereas "sullen" means bad-tempered and gloomy. The other options are either similar in meaning to "sullen" or do not directly oppose its meaning.

19. Antonyms

Pick the word opposite in meaning to the word “OBLIGATORY”.
Options:
  • A. Compulsory
  • B. Exclusionary
  • C. Voluntary
  • D. Exemplary
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: "Obligatory" means something that is required or mandatory. The opposite of obligatory is voluntary, which means something done by one's own free will without any obligation.

"Indifferent" implies a lack of interest, which does not fit the context as closely.
